About Event

CityHack is back! CityHack 2021 is a 48-hour Hackathon organized by CityU students. This year, our theme would be related to Covid-19 solutions. From January 30 to 31, we welcome all students from CityU or other local universities to work together and develop innovative products to adress the given problems using cutting-edge technologies.

FAQ
1. I'm not a CityU student, Can I register?

Sure. We welcome all Hong Kong undergraduate and postgraduate students to register our contest.

2. Will the meals be covered in the two days?

The lunch and dinner in Jan 26 and the lunch in Jan 27 will be covered by the ogranizers.

3.Do I have to stay in the place for the whole time from 9 am 26th to 7 pm 27th? I have another competition to participate.

Ideally you are expected to participate throughout the weekend. But it is acceptable to work remotely. You have to join the presentation and award ceremony from 3:00pm to 5:30pm on 27th. The food will be covered if you decide to stay at the venue.

4. Do I have to form a group? May I work individual or find a group from the place?

It is preferred to form a group, but again, it is optional. If you register individually, we will arrange you into a group based on your role preference. But for better communication, you are advised to form a group before the hackathon.

5. What is the topic and expectation for this competition?

The topic would be related to AI, but the detailed information will be announced during the opening ceremony. The expected outcome of the competition would be a product (e.g. Website, Mobile APP) or a solution (e.g. a predictive model, a pipeline) to the problem. There will be a technical staff on stage during the hackathon event to answer your questions regarding the technical issues.

6. If I form a group, does he have to be an undergraduate CityU student or he can be post-graduate or a student from a different university?

We don't have any strict limitations on the identity of the contestants unless he/she is the organizer/sponsor to this event. In fact, many registered contestants are not from CityU.

7. May I present and develop something I already have?

No. Your submitted project must be built during the hackathon.
